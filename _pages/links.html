Links

The following links are reference material and examples of what different governments are using to deliver business cases. There are a variety of frameworks and templates that you can use based on the requirements and complexity of your ICT business case. You should use this information as a guide.

You will need to determine the most appropriate frameworks and documents that meet your needs to deliver the business case. Your organisation or government may already have resources designed to meet your specific needs.

Agile and Government services: an introduction (UK) - Introduction to how some organisations approach different implementation methods.

Annual Report on Major Projects 2017-18 (UK) - Example of how to illustrate programs of work to costs and benefits associated with them.

Assurance Reviews Process Overview (Australia) - This is a guide on assurance practices that help capture important considerations in program delivery.

Better Business Cases (UK) - Detailed example on the steps some organisations provide to build a business case.

Business Case Canvas (Estonia) - Estonia has adopted a business canvas approach for reviewing and prioritising ICT projects. This presents the key element of the business case in a one page (A4) format.

Business Case Options Framework (New Zealand) – The options framework provides a structured approach to identifying and filtering a broad range of options for delivering policies, strategies, programmes and projects.

Business Case Template (First Pass) (Australia) - Provides an example of the detail required by some organisations to consider a major project proposal.

Commonwealth Investments Resource Management Guide (Australia) - Detailed guidance on how the Resource Management Guide can be used in the development of a business case.

Concept Case (Canada) – This document is used to describe a business problem or opportunity and the conceptual future state and intended outcome(s) that could result from a potential digital project.

Cost-Benefit Analysis (Australia) - Guide on why it is important to gather the right information as part of the cost-benefit analysis.

Cost Estimation Guide (Australia) – This is a guide to allow robust costing to occur as there are a number of principles that should be covered when they are estimated.

Detailed business case of preferred option (Australia) - This document provides guidance on the steps and the detail proposed to justify the preferred option.

Developing a Business Case (Australia) - Guidance on the steps involved in developing a business case.

Digital Service Standard Criteria (Australia) – Provides detail across Australia’s Digital Service Standard criteria which is made up of 13 best practice principles for designing and delivering government services.

ICT Business Case Guide (Australia) - Detailed guidance on how some organisations would like business cases developed and the steps involved.

ICT Risk Management Guidance (New Zealand) - This document goes through the steps for assessing risk and measuring it in your business case.

The ICT Commissioning Playbook (UK) - Guidance on how to do an ICT procurement activity.

Risk analysis in Regulation Impact Statements guidance document (Australia) - Detailed guidance on how to use risk analysis as part of the business case.

Stakeholder Mapping and Analysis Tool (Australia) - This is an example of how some organisations may identify the key stakeholders required to be engaged in the development of a business case.
