The business case is a living document. The decision-makers have considered the options in your business case and agreed to a way forward. The work you have done to assess the costs, benefits, risks and implementation details of your preferred solution will continue to inform the project or program of work that now commences.

This Play will help you use the business case to commence your project or program to solve your business problem, and then successfully deliver an effective solution.

{% include play-section-end.html section-title="introduction" %} {% include play-section-start.html section-title="playDescription" %}

Once a business case has been approved, it becomes both the basis for implementing your change and for checking your progress.

{% include play-section-end.html section-title="playDescription" %} {% include play-section-start.html section-title="keyActivities" %}

Consider when you will review the business case

Schedule reviews of the business case as part of your project planning. For example, in a traditional waterfall-managed project, you might review the business case at the end of a project phase. you might schedule a formal review as part of your retrospective at the end of a sprint or run of sprint.
