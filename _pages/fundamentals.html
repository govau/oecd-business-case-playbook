Fundamentals

There are five key fundamentals for building a better business case. These are the ground rules that underpin each play. They will enable you to succeed in developing a business case for your digital transformation or ICT investment proposal. Click the links to discover the plays each fundamental practice and process supports.

 

ESTABLISH A GOVERNANCE FRAMEWORK

Appoint an executive sponsor to gain strong engagement. This will provide top down support that ensures decisions relating to the business case are made by the right people, at the right time.

Refer to: Play 0, Play 1, Play 5

PLAN YOUR TIME

Set realistic timeframes to develop the business case and schedule key activities including appropriate lead times for review and decision points.

Refer to: Play 1, Play 6, Play 7

ENGAGE EARLY AND OFTEN WITH YOUR STAKEHOLDERS

A collaborative approach with your stakeholders will bring them on the journey and provide useful insights.

Refer to: Play 0, Play 4, Play 5, Play 7

OBTAIN THE INFORMATION YOU NEED TO SUPPORT YOUR PROPOSAL

Ensure the team has access to the right information to develop the business case. Keep in mind your government may have its own requirements to making investment decisions.

Refer to: Play 1, Play 2, Play 3, Play 4, Play 7

TEST AND ITERATE

Develop, test and revise the business case regularly . The business case needs to be a persuasive story that brings together the views of many stakeholders. Remember the business case is a live document. You will learn more as you progress!

Refer to: Play 0, Play 1, Play 4, Play 5, Play 7
