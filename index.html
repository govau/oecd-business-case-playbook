Developing better business cases

The purpose of this Playbook is to help countries develop business cases which support investment decisions in digital transformation and ICT.

The Playbook explores what works, and what does not. It is based on the experiences of Australia, with contributions from Denmark, Canada, Estonia and the United Kingdom.

The Playbook covers foundational concepts of a business case required to present a compelling argument for a digital or ICT investment. Each Play explores a core component of business case development, supported by helpful links.

What is a business case?

A business case is a framework which captures the justification for initiating a program or project as well as options to solve the identified problems.

A business case should give the decision-maker confidence the preferred option has undergone rigorous analysis to ensure it will achieve the intended outcomes.

Why is a business case important?

There is no one-size-fits-all to digital transformation. Different organisations will be at different stages of their journey.

How you invest in digital transformation will depend on where your organisation is in that journey, and how it fits with the broader plans of your government.
