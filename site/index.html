Introduction - Purpose

The purpose of this Playbook is to help government agencies identify and adopt best practice in building a business case for public sector transformation, while adopting digital technologies and approaches.

While principles-based, it lists the mandatory requirements when constructing a Digital or ICT-enabled business case, with a focus on building a compelling argument for the proposal to be approved and funded.

Positioning for Success

  • Understand and/or establish a governance framework, including executive sponsor support to ensure timely decision making on critical issues / dependencies and the appropriate levels of sign-off are achieved both internally and externally (e.g. Central Agencies).
  • Engage early and frequently with central and partner agencies to gain support for the Government submission.
  • Ensure the business case team has access to the right information and inputs to develop the cost and benefits models, with reference to the relevant budget process and operations
  • Set realistic timeframes and scheduling for business case delivery and review points.
  • Ensure on-going engagement with key stakeholders to ensure a collaborative approach is taken and support is maintained.
  • Obtain stakeholder consensus on the strategic narrative and iterate as required, ensuring changes are applied throughout business case as required.

Play 0: Understand the context

Assemble a multi-disciplinary Team (MDT)

Actions

  • Undertake discovery and research.
  • Make sure that you have a good understanding of the business and policy problems you are trying to address.
  • Understand user needs and wants – both for the specific service and in the broader context.
  • Quantify the issues – i.e. develop baseline metrics of our benefits.

Play 1: Setting the strategic context

Identify and outline the problem/opportunity that will be addressed by the business case. It should identify the area or areas where there are issues which should be addressed, such as inefficiencies and unfavourable service delivery outcomes. This should consider the broader operational context including the strategic policy which is delivered by the organisation.

Actions

Steps

  1. Define the business problem and supporting drivers for change.
  2. Identify the key stakeholder group impacted.
  3. Establish consensus of business need across identified stakeholder groups.
  4. Develop an overview of the current state environment for the organisation, including strategic priorities and policy context.
  5. Articulate the impact of maintaining the status quo, supported by evidence.

Questions/Considerations

  • What issues needs to be addressed in the current environment?
  • What is the problem that the business case is proposing to solve?
  • What is the context within which the problem occurs?
  • What are the organisations strategic priorities?
  • How big is the problem?
  • Who are the stakeholders impacted?
  • What metrics can be used to demonstrate the magnitude of the problem

Play 8: How to manage implementation

The implementation approach needs to be managed from the point of inception. Business case complexity may have an impact on the range of implementation approaches available for consideration

Actions

  • Governance / quality / assurance
  • Scope
  • Schedule
  • Risks
  • Benefits
  • Technology design
  • Procurement
  • Change (training and comms)
  • Comms and stakeholder
  • Agile methodolgy

Steps

  1. Start thinking about implementation early – use an agile methodology to design and develop an implementation approach.
  2. An agile implementation approach is contingent on a range of factors including governance, assurance, risk, benefits realisation, cyber security, human resources, and costings, change management, stakeholder engagement.

Questions/Considerations

  • What is your team’s capability and capacity to deliver the preferred solution?
  • Does your team have a Reviewer with practical experience of delivery using agile approaches to effectively verify the robustness of the project design and delivery?
  • How will you implement the ICT project component within your entity architecture?
  • How will your new option/solution interact with existing, legacy or external systems?
  • What is the current state of the ICT system? How will this impact the implementation of your solution?
  • Does your ICT solution align with the DTA Digital Service Standard?
